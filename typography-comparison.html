Typography — Lato (PROD) and alternatives

Production uses Lato for most UI and body copy. Each article below is the same hierarchy (h1–h3, paragraphs, links) in a different family. Scopri di più appears as small-caps text, as a button-shaped link, or both—so you can judge rhythm in context.

Why compare?

Lato remains a solid, neutral humanist sans. The first columns stay in a similar sans bracket (UI / geometric / rounded). The last three step away on purpose: IBM Plex Sans (engineering clarity), Merriweather (transitional serif, editorial), Space Grotesk (tight geometric, “tech lab” rhythm). All load from Google Fonts for this static page only.

Eight families (sans cluster + contrast voices) — 4 + 4 in two rows

Lato is the live baseline. IBM Plex Sans through Outfit are sans options still in a familiar product / marketing bracket. Merriweather (serif) and Space Grotesk (compact geo-tech) are the strongest visual breaks; IBM Plex is the lighter “off-ramp” from the rounded-grotesque cluster.

  • Lato Current PROD: humanist, neutral, versatile body + UI.
  • IBM Plex Sans Contrast Replaces Source Sans 3: IBM’s family—crisper, more “lab / catalog” than Lato; not another Adobe humanist twin.
  • DM Sans Geometric but warm—fresh silhouette without display excess.
  • Plus Jakarta Sans Slightly rounded technical sans; common on SaaS and cultural sites.
  • Manrope Grotesque with subtle rounding; strong at headings and short labels.
  • Outfit Clean geometric with a contemporary poster feel; good display + UI pairing.
  • Merriweather Contrast Transitional serif: long-form reading, “pubblicazione” tone—pair with a sans for UI if you mix.
  • Space Grotesk Contrast Compact geometric with a monospace-adjacent vibe; sharp and cool vs. rounded sans.
